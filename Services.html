Student Activities

Cultural Activities & Youth Welfare

DAV Hoshiarpur is celebrated for all-round personality development. Students don't just study here — they perform, compete, celebrate, and grow. From the Annual Talent Hunt to Zonal Youth Festivals, every student finds their stage.

Youth Welfare Aim: To promote culture, sports, social service, and fellowship. Students regularly bring home Individual and Group prizes from inter-college competitions.

The Youth Welfare program organizes celebrations like Teej and Basant, alongside intra and inter-college competitions through the year.

National Service Scheme

Not Me, But You

NSS enrollment is offered at the time of admission. The unit at DAV Hoshiarpur is known for its active community engagement year-round.

Social awareness lectures and outreach programs
Cleanliness Drives across campus and nearby areas
Blood Donation Camps organized every year
Rural Camps — immersive community service
National Youth Day celebrations
Seminars and rallies against drug abuse

National Cadet Corps

Unity and Discipline

The NCC unit conducts an orientation programme at the start of every session. Cadets can appear for 'B' and 'C' certificate examinations — a major advantage for armed forces aspirants.

Orientation Programme each academic session
'B' and 'C' Certificate examinations
Blood Donation drives
Tree Plantation drives under Green India
Swachh Bharat Abhiyan participation
Discipline and leadership training camps

🎗 Red Ribbon Club — The Red Ribbon Club works alongside NSS to spread awareness about HIV/AIDS, blood donation, and health sensitization among students and the wider community.

The cell also organizes career-oriented sessions, panel discussions with industry professionals, and orientation programs to help students make informed choices about their futures.

Anti-Ragging Cell

🚫

Zero Tolerance Policy

DAV College Hoshiarpur has a strict zero-tolerance policy against ragging in any form. Any act of ragging is a punishable offence under UGC regulations. Students and parents are encouraged to report any incident immediately.

🔗 UGC Anti-Ragging Guidelines ↗
What Counts as Ragging?
  • Verbal abuse, humiliation, or intimidation of any student
  • Physical harm or forced acts of any nature
  • Any activity that causes mental distress to a fellow student
  • Online harassment or cyber-bullying on any platform

Complaints can also be lodged at the national anti-ragging helpline: 1800-180-5522
